The Opportunity

Market Momentum

  • Third-person action-adventure is the dominant premium genre, with proven commercial success across mythological settings
  • God of War Ragnarök: 15M+ copies. Black Myth Wukong: 20M+ in one month. Kena: 2.5M+
  • Players are actively seeking fresh, culturally distinctive worlds
  • African mythology is the largest untapped mythological foundation in AAA/AA gaming

The Gap

  • Norse, Greek, Japanese, and Chinese mythology have all been explored extensively
  • No major AAA or AA title has explored West African folklore as its core foundation
  • A massive, underserved audience exists globally, including the African diaspora and mythology enthusiasts
  • First-mover advantage in defining this space

The Story

  • During a royal festival, the kingdom's storyteller is murdered mid-performance
  • His unfinished tale tears open the boundary between the human and spirit worlds
  • Kweku, an apprentice griot and petty thief, is marked by Anansi the trickster spirit
  • He must recover scattered tale fragments before a masked ruler uses stolen spiritual authority to remake the kingdom
  • Player choices shape alliances, trust, and which ending they reach

Core fantasy: Not the strongest warrior. Not the chosen king.
The survivor who wins through wit.

Characters

The Protagonist

Kweku

Clever, underestimated apprentice storyteller. Quick with words, fast on his feet. His arc explores the tension between deception for survival and deception for power.

The Trickster Spirit

Anansi

Charismatic ancient trickster. Neither fully benevolent nor malicious. Empowers Kweku while constantly testing his character and resolve.

The Hunter-Priestess

Ama Serwaa

Disciplined, capable, fundamentally at odds with Kweku's methods. She is the story's emotional anchor and a formidable ally.

The Antagonist

Nana Bekoe

The Masked Regent. Uses fear, ritual theater, and stolen spiritual authority to control the fractured kingdom. The political enemy.

🎞

Core Mechanics

Combat

Third-person melee with dodge, counter, and gadget-like spirit abilities. Rewards cleverness, not brute force. Enemies require observation and adaptation.

Stealth

Infiltrate cities, shrines, and forests. Social stealth with voice mimicry and disguise. Avoid, deceive, or manipulate rather than fight.

Traversal

Rooftops, trees, cliff faces, web-swinging, and spirit-thread pathways. Fluid, vertical movement through diverse terrain.

Puzzles

Voice mimicry, Adinkra symbol interpretation, rhythm challenges, memory sequences, and proverb-based gates. Culturally rooted and mechanically varied.

Narrative Choices

Dialogue, alliances, and moral decisions shape the story. Multiple endings based on accumulated choices. Trust is a resource.

Art & Audio Identity

Visual Style

Ghanaian textiles, carved wood motifs, gold regalia, Adinkra symbolic design, and luminous spirit-thread effects. Every visual element is culturally grounded.

Corruption Language

Woven shadows, cracked symbols, impossible geometry. Corruption is expressed through broken craft and unraveled pattern, not generic smoke or darkness.

Music & Sound

Layered percussion, call-and-response vocals, ritual rhythms, region-specific instrumentation. An original score rooted in West African musical traditions.

Music as Gameplay

Some enemies and puzzles are rhythm-sensitive. Music is not just atmosphere; it is a mechanical layer that rewards attentive players.

Culturally grounded. Visually distinct. Highly marketable.
